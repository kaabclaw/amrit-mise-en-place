Beach &amp; Pool Attendant Guest Service Duties (Ongoing)
Checklist Recreation
▾

⚠️ Important Rules

  • All upsell recommendations must feel like genuine service enhancements — never a hard sell.
  • Never deflect or argue during a complaint — always acknowledge, empathize, and own.
  • Escalate complaints to supervisor when resolution is beyond your authority.
  • Answer all calls within three rings and respond to email/WhatsApp within 2 hours.
  • All written communication must be professional, warm, and grammatically correct.
  • Section 10 — Experience Enhancement (Upsell & Retail)
  • Service Add-On Offer
  • Script: "To enhance your experience, many of our guests enjoy adding [service]. May I include that for you today?"
  • Wellness Recommendation
  • Script: "To complement your treatment, I would recommend [service] to extend the benefits."
  • Retail Departure Recommendation
  • Script: "Based on your experience today, I recommend [product] to continue the benefits at home. I am happy to share more about how it works."
  • Retail Display Standards
  • Retail display organization is a daily responsibility of the Spa Concierge.
  • Displays must always be clean, fully stocked, and visually inviting — never depleted or disorganized.
  • Product knowledge is expected: team members must speak to every product in the retail display.
  • During downtime, organize, restock, and refresh retail displays proactively.
  • Section 11 — Service Recovery Standards
  • Service recovery is a defining moment of a luxury brand. Handle every recovery situation with calm ownership and no defensiveness.
  • Handling Delays
  • Script: "Thank you for your patience, Mr./Ms. [Last Name]. We will have everything ready for you shortly."
  • Script: "I sincerely apologize for the delay. We are preparing your experience to the highest standard."
  • Complaint Handling — 4-Step Recovery Model
  • Acknowledge: "Thank you for sharing this with me." — Receive the complaint without interruption. Acknowledgment alone can de-escalate most situations.
  • Empathize: "I completely understand, and I am genuinely sorry to hear this." — Validate the guest's experience. Never argue, justify, or deflect.
  • Take Ownership: "Please allow me to take care of this for you personally." — Step into the role of problem solver. The guest should feel they have a champion.
  • Resolve: "I will ensure this is handled immediately and to your full satisfaction." — Provide a clear, tangible next step. Escalate to supervisor if resolution is beyond your authority.
  • Critical rule: Never deflect. Never argue. Escalate to supervisor when a resolution is beyond your authority — and inform the guest that the matter is being elevated, not dismissed.
  • Section 12 — Phone & Written Communication Standards
  • Answering the Phone
  • Script: "Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Amrit Wellness & Spa. This is [Name], how may I assist you?"
  • Placing on Hold
  • Script: "May I place you on a brief hold while I look into that for you?"
  • Returning from Hold
  • Script: "Thank you so much for your patience."
  • Closing a Call
  • Script: "Is there anything else I may assist you with today? Thank you for calling Amrit Wellness & Spa. We look forward to welcoming you."
  • Phone Rules
  • Answer all calls within three rings.
  • Never place a guest on hold for more than 60 seconds without returning to check in.
  • Always introduce yourself by name.
  • Maintain the same warm, professional tone on the phone as in person.
  • Email & WhatsApp Standards
  • Opening line for all written communication: "Good afternoon, Mr./Ms. [Last Name]. Thank you for reaching out to Amrit Wellness & Spa. I would be happy to assist you."
  • All written communication must be professional, warm, and grammatically correct — no abbreviations, no slang.
  • Response time: within 2 hours during operating hours for all email and WhatsApp inquiries.
  • Proofread every message before sending — spelling errors are not acceptable.
  • Use guest names in every written communication.
  • Section 13 — Guest Tours
  • Guest tours are both a hospitality service and a conversion opportunity. A well-conducted tour builds confidence in the facility and naturally opens the door to service upgrades.
  • Tour Standards
  • Tours are conducted when staffing allows and lobby coverage is fully maintained.
  • The Spa Concierge leads all tours; attendants may assist only when the concierge is unavailable.
  • Maintain a luxury pace throughout — never rushed, never hurried.
  • Engage the guest with the story of each space — Ayush traditions, hydrotherapy circuit, the integration of movement and stillness.
  • Always close the tour with an invitation to book or enhance a service.
  • Tour Opening Language
  • Script: "Allow me to guide you through our wellness facilities so you can fully appreciate your experience today."
  • Key Areas to Highlight
  • Locker facilities and amenity overview
  • Hydro and Ayush facilities — explain the circuit and benefits
  • Relaxation spaces — highlight the sense of calm and intentional design
  • Integrated wellness programming and active wellness section
  • Treatment areas (where appropriate, without entering active treatment zones)
  • 📷 Concierge leading guest tour — open welcoming gesture toward facility, professional posture, guest engaged

Upselling at Amrit Wellness & Spa is not a sales exercise — it is a service enhancement. Every recommendation must come from a genuine place of adding value to the guest's wellness journey.

  • Operational Reference
  • Seating Rotation & Server Flow
  • Purpose: To ensure every server maintains a balanced table load and guests receive attentive, unrushed service throughout the shift.
  • How it works: The host controls the pace and fairness of service by rotating table assignments in sequence rather than filling one section at a time.
  • Rotation Protocol
  • Tables are assigned to servers in rotation order, one table at a time
  • The host must never seat two consecutive tables in the same server's section unless that server has fewer than the floor average
  • When a server is running behind (has just been sat, is at the table, or has a large party), the host skips that server and moves to the next in rotation
  • The host communicates with the floor manager if any server's section is becoming unmanageable
  • Station Awareness
  • Before seating any guest, the host confirms: has the previous table in that section been greeted? If not — hold the seat
  • Priority sections (window, ocean-facing, corner booths) rotate equally — no server holds these exclusively
  • Large parties (6+) are pre-assigned by the manager on duty, not by standard rotation
  • Communication Standard
  • Host calls out each seating to the server directly: "[Server name], table [number], [party size]"
  • Server acknowledges verbally before host walks guests to the table
  • If server does not acknowledge within 10 seconds, host alerts the floor manager before seating
  • Goal: Every guest sits into a section where the server is ready. No guest waits ungreeted because a section was over-sat.
